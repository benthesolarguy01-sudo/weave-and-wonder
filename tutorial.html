How to Make a Friendship Bracelet

Your step-by-step guide from Weave & Wonder

What's In Your Kit

Before you start, check you have everything:

  • 1 x Tasmanian Oak base board (30cm x 6.5cm) with our logo heat-stamped on
  • 2 x Wooden dowels (13cm tall) — they slot right in!
  • 6 x Wax thread colours (4.6m each) — Starter Kit, or 12 colours in the Rainbow Kit
  • Pattern cards with illustrated designs
  • Scissors — the only thing you need from home!
1

Set Up Your Loom

Take the two wooden dowels and push them into the holes on your Tasmanian Oak base board. They should stand up nice and straight — no glue needed! Place your loom on a flat surface in front of you.

Tip: The dowels should feel snug. Give them a gentle twist as you push them in.
2

Choose Your Colours & Cut

Pick 3 to 6 thread colours for your bracelet. Cut each piece about 60cm long (roughly from your fingertip to your opposite shoulder). Gather all your threads together and tie them in a knot at one end, leaving a 5cm tail.

Tip: Start with 3-4 colours for your first bracelet — it's easier to learn the pattern!
3

Attach to Your Loom

Loop the knotted end around one of the dowels and pull it snug. Fan out your threads so each colour is separate and easy to grab. Arrange them side by side in the order you want your pattern to go.

Tip: The order of your colours from left to right is the order of your stripes!
4

Make Your First Knot

Take the thread on the far left. This is your "working thread."

A. Cross it OVER the thread next to it, making a shape like the number 4.

B. Tuck the end under the second thread and pull it through the loop.

C. Pull it tight toward the top. Do this same knot TWICE on the same thread — that's the secret to keeping it neat!

Remember: Over, under, pull — that's all a forward knot is! Always do it twice.
5

Work Across the Row

Using that same first thread (your working thread), make your double-knot on the next thread, and the next, all the way across until you reach the other side.

You've just completed one row! The thread that started on the left is now on the far right.

Tip: Keep your tension even — not too tight, not too loose. The knots should sit neatly in a line.
6

Keep Going — Row After Row

Now pick up whichever thread is on the far left again and repeat! Double-knot across every thread, left to right.

Each row creates a beautiful diagonal stripe of colour. You'll start to see your pattern forming after just 3-4 rows.

Tip: If you make a mistake, don't worry! You can gently undo the last knot with a pin or needle. Everyone makes mistakes when learning.
7

Use Your Loom

As your bracelet gets longer, use the second dowel to hold it taut. Wrap your growing bracelet around the dowels to keep the tension even and give yourself room to keep knotting.

The loom makes this so much easier than holding it by hand!

8

Finish & Tie Off

When your bracelet is long enough to wrap around your wrist (usually about 15-18cm), carefully remove it from the loom. Tie all the threads together in a tight knot.

Trim the ends to about 3cm and plait them into a little braid for a neat finish. Tie it on a friend's wrist and make their day!

Tip: Measure against your wrist as you go — make it a little longer since you need room for the knot!

Try These Patterns!

Candy Stripe

The classic! Use 3-4 colours and knot left to right. The easiest pattern to start with.

Chevron

Mirror your threads and knot from the outside in. Creates beautiful V shapes!

Diamond

Combine forward and backward knots to create diamond shapes. Intermediate level!

Troubleshooting

My bracelet is twisting! — Make sure you're always knotting in the same direction (left to right) and doing each knot twice.

My knots look uneven! — Practice makes perfect! Keep your tension consistent and pull each knot to the same tightness.

I ran out of thread! — You can tie a new piece to the end of the short thread with a small knot. Tuck the ends behind your work.

I made a mistake! — Use a pin or needle to gently loosen the knot and undo it. Take your time!
